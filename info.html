Strategic Voting & Party Policies

What is Strategic Voting?

Strategic voting is when a voter chooses a candidate not because they are their first choice, but to prevent another candidate from winning. This can be useful in close elections where small shifts in votes can determine the outcome.

Why It Matters

Your vote can help block an unpopular candidate from winning or support a party that aligns more with your values. In tight races, voting strategically can make a difference.

General Breakdown of Party Policies

  • Party A: Focuses on tax cuts, private sector growth, and reduced government spending.
  • Party B: Supports a balanced approach to social programs, moderate taxation, and business incentives.
  • Party C: Advocates for increased government services, social welfare, and environmental policies.
